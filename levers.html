A little bit about why levers are needed for drifting?
These levers initially have settings adapted to drift.
Due to shortened knuckle levers (bipods) we significantly reduce the Ackerman angle,
which has a decent value on the stock levers and does not allow the car to accelerate decently in a skid.
The increased camber and castor angles also allow for much better control of the car in a skid.
The pilot begins to tell the car where and how he wants to go, and not to turn around.
